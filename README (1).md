# DCLED-Dynamic-Confidence-Aware-Layer-wise-Evolution-Decoding
Large language models frequently generate factually incorrect outputs despite their impressive capabilities. We introduce DCLED (Dynamic Confidence-Aware Layer-wise Evolution Decoding), an inference-time method that enhances factual accuracy without retraining or external data. DCLED integrates three novel mechanisms: (1) iterative gradient-based logit evolution using early-layer contrastive anchors, (2) confidence-aware gating that selectively applies refinement only to uncertain predictions, and (3) dynamic confidence boosting that adapts correction strength based on probability concentration. Across TruthfulQA, HotpotQA, and SEAL benchmarks using Llama models (1B-8B parameters), DCLED achieves up to 48\% relative improvement over vanilla decoding on multi-hop reasoning tasks, with smaller models benefiting most substantially. Our method introduces 14-160\% latency overhead versus SLED baseline, with costs dominated by iterative logit evolution. Systematic ablations reveal that confidence gating is critical for complex reasoning (contributing 16-18 points on HotpotQA), while contrastive direction enhancement is essential for distractor-heavy tasks. DCLED provides a practical, training-free approach for deploying more factually reliable language models.
